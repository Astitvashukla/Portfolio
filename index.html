My Evolving Journey: From Diverse Experiences to Product Focus

My career journey has been anything but linear — and that's my greatest strength. I've worn multiple hats across fintech, edtech, sales, live operations, and quality assurance, each chapter contributing to the customer-centric and analytical mindset I bring to product thinking today.

I began my professional path in highly customer-facing environments — at Domino's and later at BYJU'S — where I developed a sharp instinct for what people value, how they interact with products in real time, and what it takes to deliver experiences that are both seamless and memorable. These roles grounded me in empathy, urgency, and the art of listening to feedback beyond just metrics.

My transition into Quality Assurance roles at Novo (a modern fintech platform) and Frontrow (a creator-led edtech company) marked a shift toward technical ownership and system-level thinking. Here, I embraced responsibilities that spanned manual testing, API validation, and test automation. I collaborated closely with product and engineering teams, driving iterative improvements powered by real user feedback and data. Tools like Mixpanel, Postman, Cypress, and customer voice channels became central to my workflow. I wasn't just testing features — I was advocating for user trust and performance at every release.

What sets my journey apart is the ability to connect dots between user behavior, business impact, and technical feasibility. Whether it was refining a feature rollout based on customer pain points, or identifying patterns in support tickets that informed backlog priorities, I was always playing the role of a translator between users and builders — long before I formally wore the PM hat.

Today, as I pivot into Product Management, I bring with me:

  • Firsthand knowledge of customer journeys and user pain points
  • A deep appreciation for clean, testable design
  • Proficiency in using data and analytics tools to guide decisions
  • The ability to work across cross-functional teams and communicate with clarity

My curiosity has also been amplified by observing the rise of AI — not just as a technology trend but as a product challenge and opportunity. I've approached this space like a journalist: exploring, questioning, and analyzing how AI can create meaningful utility for real people.

In essence, my journey reflects a deep-rooted passion for building products that are grounded in empathy, engineered with care, and aligned with strategic impact.
